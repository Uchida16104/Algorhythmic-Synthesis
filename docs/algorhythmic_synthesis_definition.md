# Algorhythmic Synthesis

## English Definition
**Algorhythmic Synthesis** is a revolutionary musical genre that integrates mathematical structures (differential calculus, integral calculus, polar coordinates, complex equations, parametric equations, matrices, vectors, limits, sequences, Fibonacci sequences, Markov chains, chaos theory, Lorenz equations, partial differential equations), physical phenomena (Doppler effect, spectral analysis), and comprehensive musical history from Gregorian chant to contemporary techniques.

The genre employs:
- **Medieval techniques**: Organum, clausula, conductus, sequentia
- **Renaissance forms**: Virelai, rondeau, ballade, isorhythm, ballata, motet, homophony
- **Baroque structures**: Basso continuo, counterpoint, chanson, monodic style, aria, ritornello form, canon, fugue
- **Classical forms**: Sonata form, sonatina form, rondo-sonata form, grand rondo, petit rondo
- **Modern innovations**: Total serialism, graphic notation, variation forms, minimalism, aleatoric music, twelve-tone technique, pitch bend, ring modulator, polytempo, polyrhythm

Combined with literary poetry, visual arts (Art Deco, Art Brut, Impressionism, Avant-garde), contemporary dance, and comedic timing structures.
